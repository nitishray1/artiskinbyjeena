Behind the Session 4 min read

What actually happens in an ArtiSkin session — from the first message to your personalised plan

If you've never had a proper skin consultation — or the last one felt like a sales appointment — here is exactly what to expect from ArtiSkin. No surprises. No pressure. No jargon.

If you've never had a proper skin consultation before — or you've had one that felt more like a sales pitch than a genuine conversation — it's completely normal to feel a bit unsure about what you're walking into. This article walks you through exactly what happens in an ArtiSkin session, from your first message to the moment you have a personalised plan in hand.

Step one: The first message

Everything starts with a WhatsApp message to Jeena — just your name, your suburb, and what you're hoping to work on. Jeena responds personally. There's no lengthy booking form, no upfront payment, no commitment required.

Jeena will reply quickly — usually within a few hours — to introduce herself, ask any questions she needs to understand your situation, and confirm a time that works for you. She'll also confirm whether she's coming to you (within 20km of Merrylands) or whether you'd prefer to visit her at home.

If you're not sure which service to book, that's fine. Jeena will help you figure that out in the conversation.

Step two: Before you arrive (or before Jeena arrives)

There's nothing complicated you need to do. Come with clean or naturally clean skin if possible — no heavy makeup, no freshly applied SPF immediately before the skin assessment. That said, Jeena can work around whatever state your skin is in. If you have any skin concerns, products you're currently using, or medical conditions relevant to your skin (eczema, rosacea, active breakouts), it's worth having that in mind to mention.

For mobile appointments, Jeena brings everything she needs. She'll set up in a space with good natural light if possible, and take it from there.

Step three: The conversation

Before anything goes near your skin, Jeena sits down and has a proper conversation. This is the part most clients say they didn't expect — how genuinely listening it is.

She'll ask about your lifestyle: your diet, your sleep, your stress levels, how much water you drink, whether you spend time outdoors. All of these have a direct relationship with skin health. She'll ask about your current routine, what you've tried that worked, what hasn't worked, and what you're most hoping to change.

This isn't small talk. It's a clinical intake, but delivered like a conversation. Everything you share shapes what comes next.

Step four: Skin education

Based on what Jeena has heard, she'll explain what she suspects is happening with your skin and why — before the skin assessment device is even switched on. You'll understand your skin type, what that actually means, and what conditions might be at play. Clients often describe this part as the moment things start clicking into place.

This education-first approach is deliberate. Jeena's view is that a client who understands their own skin will make better decisions for the rest of their life — not just the next product purchase.

Step five: professional skin assessment

Then comes the skin assessment device. You sit in front of the device, and within under 3 minutes, you see your skin through UV-A light analysis — as you've likely never seen it before.

Jeena walks you through everything she can see in real time: where dehydration is concentrated, where congestion is building (often in places clients didn't know about), where visible signs of UV exposure appear beneath the surface, how oil is distributing across your face. She explains what each finding means and what it tells her about what your skin needs.

This is often the most memorable part of the session. Clients leave this step understanding their skin in a fundamentally different way than when they walked in.

Step six: The treatment (if you've booked a facial)

If your booking includes a facial, this follows the skin assessment. Jeena selects the specific treatment based on what she saw — she doesn't stick rigidly to a menu if something different would better serve your skin that day. You're in a comfortable position, usually lying down, while Jeena works. She explains each step as she goes.

Facial treatments range from 45 to 90 minutes depending on the type. You can ask questions throughout. Many clients find this part deeply relaxing.

Step seven: Your personalised plan

At the end of the session, Jeena puts together your personalised skincare regime — a morning and evening routine built specifically around what she saw in your skin assessment, your skin type, your lifestyle, and your goals.

This isn't a product list. It's a complete guide: what to use, when to use it, in what order, and why. You'll understand the function of each step. There's no pressure to purchase anything — the plan works with products you already have wherever possible, and introduces recommendations only where they'd genuinely make a difference.

What happens after

Jeena follows up. At the 4-week mark, she checks in to see how your skin is responding, whether the routine is working for you, and whether anything needs to be adjusted. As your skin changes — with seasons, stress, hormones, or age — the routine can change with it.

This is the part most skincare services don't offer. And it's often the difference between a session that was a nice experience and one that actually changed your skin.

The goal isn't just for you to feel good for a day. It's for you to understand your skin well enough that every decision you make from here forward is informed. — Jeena
